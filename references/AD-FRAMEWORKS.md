# Ad Frameworks Reference

This document outlines the 6 core ad copywriting frameworks used in the GH Creative Dashboard, optimized for the Philippine market with Taglish examples.

## 1. PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
- **When to use:** Pain-point driven products, course launches, when the audience is highly aware of their struggle.
- **Structure Template:** 
  1. Identify the problem (Hook)
  2. Amplify the pain (Why it hurts / what it costs them)
  3. Present the solution (Your product/offer)
- **FB vs IG Notes:** On FB, keep the agitation paragraph separated by line breaks. On IG, agitation works well as text overlays on B-roll video.
- **Grace Taglish Example:** "Pagod ka na ba sa 9-5? (Problem) Ang hirap kumita ng pera habang naka-bahay lang, lalo na kung wala kang tech skills at nauubos ang oras mo sa commute... (Agitate) With Papers to Profits, step-by-step mo lang gagawin, you can start earning online without the tech overwhelm. (Solution)"

## 2. AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
- **When to use:** Cold audiences, top-of-funnel awareness campaigns.
- **Structure Template:**
  1. Hook (Attention)
  2. Educate/Fascinate (Interest)
  3. Create want/FOMO (Desire)
  4. Call to action (Action)
- **FB vs IG Notes:** FB allows for longer "Interest" sections (storytelling). IG requires visually keeping the Interest and Desire moving quickly (under 15 seconds).
- **Grace Taglish Example:** "Did you know na pwede kang kumita habang natutulog? (Attention) Maraming mommies are now building passive income streams using digital products. (Interest) Imagine waking up to sales notifications sa phone mo, hindi mo na kailangan mag-hustle 24/7. (Desire) Click the link to join my free masterclass tonight! (Action)"

## 3. Before/After/Bridge
- **When to use:** Transformation-focused offers, testimonials, coaching programs.
- **Structure Template:**
  1. Show current state (Before)
  2. Show dream state (After)
  3. Your product is the bridge
- **FB vs IG Notes:** Very effective as Carousel posts on FB/IG where slide 1 is "Before", slide 2 is "After", and slide 3 is the "Bridge" offer.
- **Grace Taglish Example:** "Dati, struggle ko kung paano mag-budget sa maliit na sweldo. (Before) Ngayon, I have multiple income streams and I can afford to travel with my family anytime. (After) Ang secret ko? The Digital Product Blueprint. I'll show you exactly how I did it. (Bridge)"

## 4. Social Proof / Testimonial
- **When to use:** Mid-funnel, retargeting, building trust and authority.
- **Structure Template:**
  1. Student/Client result (Hook)
  2. Their journey/struggle (Relatable context)
  3. How your product helped (The mechanism)
- **FB vs IG Notes:** On FB, long-form storytelling with a screenshot image works best. On IG, a quick talking-head reel or aesthetic quote graphic performs better.
- **Grace Taglish Example:** "'Kumita ako ng P50k in my first month!' - Sarah, a stay-at-home mom. She started from zero, walang background sa tech. Pero because of the exact templates inside the academy, nagawa niya. Kung kaya ni Sarah, kaya mo rin."

## 5. Urgency / Scarcity
- **When to use:** Launch windows, limited time offers, bottom-of-funnel (BOFU) retargeting.
- **Structure Template:**
  1. Time/quantity limit (The constraint)
  2. What they'll miss out on (FOMO)
  3. Act now (Direct CTA)
- **FB vs IG Notes:** Use bold text and emojis on FB. On IG Stories, use the countdown sticker and strong visual cues pointing to the link.
- **Grace Taglish Example:** "Only 24 hours left before the doors close! (Urgency) Kung hindi ka sasali ngayon, you'll miss out on the early bird discount and the 3 exclusive bonuses. (Missed Value) Don't wait. Click here to secure your spot now! (Action)"

## 6. FAB (Features-Advantages-Benefits)
- **When to use:** Product comparison, detailed information ads, software/tool promotions.
- **Structure Template:**
  1. What it is (Feature)
  2. Why it's better/useful (Advantage)
  3. What it actually does for the user's life (Benefit)
- **FB vs IG Notes:** Use bullet points on FB for readability. On IG, highlight the Benefit in big text, and put Features in smaller text or in the caption.
- **Grace Taglish Example:** "Get 50+ ready-to-use Canva templates (Feature). Hindi mo na kailangan mag-design from scratch, just plug and play (Advantage). Para makapag-launch ka agad ng business mo this weekend and start getting sales! (Benefit)"
